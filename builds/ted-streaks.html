01

Start with who you're building for

TED's audience isn't one person. We mapped three distinct user mindsets, each with a different reason to return, and a different reason they'd churn without the right nudge.

I want to learn every day, but I never stick with it.

Habit-forming learner

Needs visible progress and a reason to open the app tomorrow. Streaks turn intention into a daily ritual they don't want to break.

I only have five minutes. Make it count.

Growth-oriented professional

Needs bite-sized engagement that fits a commute or coffee break. Daily nudges with curated content reduce decision fatigue.

If it's not fun, I'm not doing it.

Gen Z & Alpha

Grew up on gamified apps. Badges, milestones, and streak counters make learning feel native to how they already use their phone.

02

The gap between inspiration and habit

1 visitthen silence until the next viral talk

TED's mission is lifelong learning and continuous curiosity, but most users engage episodically. They watch a talk, feel inspired, and leave.

The problem isn't content. It's habit infrastructure: no repeatable routine, no progress signal, no lightweight reason to return tomorrow.

03

The build: TED Streaks

Introduce Streaks, a system that rewards consecutive days of engagement, turning TED from a destination into a daily ritual. Push notifications, email reminders, and progress bars make learning feel incremental without overwhelming users.

🔥

Daily streak counter

Visual tracker of consecutive engagement days, reinforced by notifications that celebrate momentum.

🎯

Personalized nudges

Tailored daily recommendations that keep streaks alive and cut choice fatigue.

🏅

Reward milestones

Badges and exclusive playlists unlocked at commitment thresholds.

04

The proof: what Duolingo's data shows

We benchmarked against the gold standard in habit design. Duolingo doesn't just count days, it taps into identity, loss aversion, and progress visibility to sustain long-term commitment.

30–40%

Higher persistence when long-streak users start seeing themselves as "streak keepers", an identity loop, not just a counter.

15%

Reduction in next-day churn when a Streak Freeze cushions the moment a user would have lost their progress.

2×

More lessons per session for users with 50+ day streaks, they don't just return, they go deeper.

05

How we'd measure success

Every recommendation needs a scoreboard. These three metrics tell us whether streaks are creating real habits, not just vanity engagement.

DRR

Daily return rate

Percentage of users returning each day. The core signal that streak-driven routines are actually working.

SCR

Streak completion rate

How consistently users maintain multi-week streaks, revealing predictable learning habits over time.

SDL

Session depth lift

More talks watched, ideas saved, and snippets explored per session after streak prompts fire.

06

Ship it

From recommendation to experiment, a phased rollout that validates the habit loop before scaling.

Design a lightweight streak tracker that logs daily actions inside the TED app.

Launch customizable reminder cues, push and email nudges tied to user preferences.

Ship themed streak challenges with milestone badges and exclusive content unlocks.

Run an A/B test, streak-enabled experience vs. current app. Measure DRR and session depth.
